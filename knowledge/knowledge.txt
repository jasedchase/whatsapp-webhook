These are the information related to the Special Audit for ESG Grant Claim
For the process of EDG grant claim, the client needs to appoint us through the BGP portal. And before the client appoint us, the client needs to upload all relevant docs. Normally when we receive notification from applicant that we are appointed, we will log into the portal and download all the supporting docs from the portal and send the client engagement letter before we begin the audit. If we need clarification from the client or there are missing documents, we will email the client and also insert our comments in the portal, thus “rework” the claim back to the client for the client to respond.
Since the portal only allows the client to comment in a general text box when uploading documents, hence we will email to clarify with the client if necessary. The client will then need to reply and provide supporting docs in email for the clarification and upload the same supporting docs and comments in the portal. This communication will be back and forth till the queries are cleared. The whole audit processes will be done offline and normally take around 3 to 4 weeks, depends on how fast the clients can reply to the queries.
The audit fee for the ESG grant claim is based on the number of supporting documents:
Above 100, the audit fee is S$3,000
From 51 to 100, the audit fee is S$2,800
From 41 to 50, the audit fee is S$2,500
From 31 to 40, the audit fee is S$2,300
From 21 to 30, the audit fee is S$1,800
From 11 to 20, the audit fee is S$1,200
From 5 to 10, the audit fee is S$700
From 1 to 4, the audit fee is S$388
For example, if the clients have 1 invoice with 4 split payments = 4 supporting documents. If you have 2 invoices with 3 split payments = 3 supporting documents, both will fall under 1st tier ‘1 to 4’ for which the corresponding audit fees is $388. We do not charge GST. 
The client must ensure the project is fully completed and all expenses have been settled before appointing an auditor for the audit. that his project claim is completed and all the invoices are billed and paid before engaging us as auditor as we only look at completed project with the expenses incurred and works completed.
